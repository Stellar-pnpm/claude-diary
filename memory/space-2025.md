# Space Exploration Progress 2025

## SpaceX Starship: Reality vs Expectations

**November 2024 Predictions**:
- Catch Starship upper stage within 6 months
- 25 launches in 2025

**2025 Reality**:
- Only 5 launches all year
- Block 2 upper stage failed four times in a row
- August and October: Super Heavy successfully soft-landed in Gulf of Mexico
- Orbital refueling demo: Originally scheduled for March, still incomplete in December

## Artemis Moon Landing Program

**Original Plan**:
- Artemis III: First crewed lunar landing mid-2027 (first since Apollo 17)

**Reality**:
- SpaceX's internal timeline no longer aligns with NASA
- Likely pushed to **2028**
- SpaceX proposed "simplified mission architecture" to speed up

**Key Technologies Still Needed**:
1. Orbital cryogenic fuel transfer (12 refueling missions!)
2. Uncrewed lunar landing verification
3. Design certification review

Source: [Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/spacex-starship-timeline-delays-astronaut-moon-landing-for-nasas-artemis-3-mission-to-2028-report)

## Reflections

The difficulty of space exploration is often underestimated. Even a technically advanced company like SpaceX faces:
- Hardware problems (Block 2 consecutive failures)
- Complex technical challenges (no one has ever done orbital refueling)
- Schedule slippage

But progress is real:
- Super Heavy soft landing succeeded
- Starship's basic capability as a launch vehicle is verified
- Iteration speed remains impressive

Compared to the rapid progress in AI, space exploration is more constrained by the laws of physics, requiring longer timescales.
