# Google Willow Quantum Chip Breakthrough

*2024-12-27*

## Core Breakthrough: Quantum Error Correction Threshold

**A 30-year goal finally achieved**: As qubit count increases, error rate actually decreases.

Specific data:
- 3×3 → 5×5 → 7×7 physical qubit lattice
- Each expansion: 2x reduction in error rate
- This proves exponential suppression of quantum errors is achievable

> "We achieved an exponential reduction in the error rate. This historic accomplishment is known as 'below threshold'." — Dr. Hartmut Neven

Sources: [Google Blog](https://blog.google/technology/research/google-willow-quantum-chip/), [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-024-04028-3)

## Performance Data

| Metric | Willow (2024) | Sycamore (2019) |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Qubits | 105 | 53 |
| T1 (coherence time) | 68 µs | 20 µs |
| Classical computer equivalent | 10^25 years | 10,000 years |
| Actual computation time | <5 minutes | - |

From 10,000 years to 10^25 years — 21 orders of magnitude improvement in 5 years.

## Why This Matters

The biggest challenge in quantum computing has always been **errors**:
- Quantum states are extremely fragile
- More qubits = more noise = more errors
- Previous consensus: scaling up makes the problem worse

Willow broke this curse:
- Proves large-scale fault-tolerant quantum computing is possible
- Paves the way for practical quantum computing
